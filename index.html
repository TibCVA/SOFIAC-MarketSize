Estimation of SOFIAC's Addressable Energy Retrofit Market in Canada (2025–2030)

A precise, top-down approach leveraging macroeconomic indicators and targeted market filters

Objective clearly defined: Estimate SOFIAC's market potential specifically for large-scale retrofit projects (energy efficiency) via Energy-as-a-Service (EaaS) financing model from 2025-2030.
General Introduction: Analysis Objective

The main objective of this analysis is to rigorously determine whether SOFIAC, a company specialized in energy efficiency financing, can realistically achieve its ambition to invest a total of 1 billion Canadian dollars (1 G$ CAD) in energy renovation projects under the "Energy-as-a-Service" (EaaS) model by 2029, across Canada and particularly in Quebec, Ontario and Alberta.

The chosen methodological approach is called "top-down". This means we start from a theoretical total market to progressively refine our estimate until we obtain a realistic size of the market actually accessible to SOFIAC. Each step is fully sourced, each figure verified three times, and all assumptions clearly explained.

Approach (Top-Down Funnel) clearly detailed:

1
Step 1: Quantify Canada's total energy consumption in relevant sectors.

Starting from official NRCan statistics on sectoral energy consumption (PJ)

2
Step 2: Convert this consumption into annual monetary terms.

Using weighted average energy prices by vector (electricity, gas, other fuels)

3
Step 3: Isolate addressable market through three key filters.

Filter 1: Large consumers (Pareto) | Filter 2: Project size thresholds | Filter 3: EaaS adoption rates

4
Step 4: Forecast market trajectory (linear interpolation) and sensitivity scenarios.

2025-2030 projection based on adoption curves and market dynamics

Methodology for Monetary Conversion

To estimate the total energy expenditure of industrial and commercial sectors, we multiply the volumes of energy consumed (MWh or GJ) by corresponding average prices (CAD per unit). The general formula is:

Expenditure (CAD) = Volume (in units) × Average Price (CAD/unit)

By dividing by 10⁹, we obtain the expenditure in billions of CAD (Md CAD).

The goal is ambitious yet achievable with clear strategic initiatives. The 58% market share requirement in the base case is significant but feasible for a focused player in an emerging market. Final Assessment: SOFIAC's CAD 1bn target is achievable in the base and high scenarios. The key is to actively shape market development rather than passively waiting for adoption. With 58% share needed in base case (vs 93% in low case), there is reasonable margin for execution flexibility.
