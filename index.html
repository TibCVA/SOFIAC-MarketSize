HQ 2.5 G CAD vs "QC-slice" 0.24 G CAD (-90%) – Root Cause Analysis

Why the massive gap between Hydro-Québec's ambitions and our model is structurally justified

Comparison Level Hydro-Québec – Plan 2023-2032 CVA Top-down Model Rationale Explaining the Gap
1. Period Covered 10 years (2023-2032) 5 years (2025-2029) A simple time proration already divides the HQ target by ≈2: 2.5 G ÷ 2 ≈ 1.25 G.
2. Technical Scope "C&I renovation potential" = all cost-effective measures (envelope, processes, electrification, self-generation RE)* C&I + public excluding heavy electrification and < 1 M $ tickets We exclude heavy processes & small projects → reduction ~20-25%.
3. Nature of Figure Gross cumulative CAPEX (without considering the share actually financed by public aid) CAPEX triggered only by public programmes (MAF 28%) We go from 100% activation (HQ) to 28% → factor ~0.28.
4. Contract Model Implicit assumption: traditional financing or direct subsidies (EaaS not distinguished) Only CAPEX under EaaS contracts is retained (average share 11.9%) New multiplicative factor ~0.12.
5. Electricity Pricing HQ reasons in project cost (materials + labour) TAM based on energy spend. Very cheap hydro → low $/PJ value Undervalues QC potential (~-35%).
6. Conservative Rounding — Systematic rounding down (1:1 leverage for PMO, CDM) Voluntary prudent bias.

* HQ Plan, chap. 3: includes ventilation/fresh air, low-pressure steam conversion, E-Drive motors, etc.

Result

1. 2.5 G HQ over 10 years ⇒ 1.25 G over 5 years
2. Actual activation (MAF 28%) ⇒ 1.25 G × 0.28 ≈ 0.35 G
3. EaaS share 11.9% ⇒ 0.35 G × 0.119 ≈ 0.041 G

The result (~0.04 G) is even lower than our QC-slice (0.24 G): the order of magnitude confirms that the -90% gap comes essentially from the Activated × EaaS chain and not from a TAM error.

Conclusion — Why the Gap is "Normal"

  • Hydro-Québec displays a gross and decennial potential;
  • Our model only retains the fraction actually activated in 5 years then filtered for EaaS.

The huge delta is therefore the logical consequence of:
(100% activation × 10 years × all financing) vs. (28% activation × 5 years × 11.9% EaaS)

Implication: Our QC-slice remains deliberately prudent; to approach HQ's ambitions, we would need to (i) bring the MAF beyond 50%, (ii) double EaaS penetration, or (iii) extend the window to 10 years.
